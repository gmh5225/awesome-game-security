SwiftShield is a Swift obfuscation tool that renames types and methods in iOS projects with random, irreversible encrypted names to hinder reverse engineering and jailbreak tweaks. It uses Apple's SourceKit to index the project like Xcode does, then safely renames symbols across the codebase, including third-party libraries when sources are unlocked. It can produce a conversion map for deobfuscating crash logs, and supports options such as ignoring public or open APIs and dry-run mode. Written primarily in Swift as a command-line tool, it is aimed at iOS developers who want to make static analysis and reverse engineering of their apps significantly harder.
